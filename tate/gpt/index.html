Remembering Rosa Parks

Rosa Parks, the "Mother of the Civil Rights Movement"

Timeline of Rosa Parks' Life

  • 1913 - Rosa Louise McCauley Parks is born on February 4th in Tuskegee, Alabama.
  • 1932 - Rosa marries Raymond Parks, a barber and civil rights activist.
  • 1943 - She becomes actively involved with the NAACP, fighting for civil rights.
  • 1955 - On December 1st, Rosa refuses to give up her seat on a segregated bus in Montgomery, Alabama, sparking the Montgomery Bus Boycott.
  • 1956 - The Supreme Court rules that bus segregation is unconstitutional, leading to the end of the Montgomery Bus Boycott.
  • 1965 - Rosa becomes the secretary of the Montgomery chapter of the Southern Christian Leadership Conference (SCLC).
  • 1999 - President Bill Clinton awards Rosa Parks the Congressional Gold Medal, the highest civilian honor.
  • 2005 - Rosa Parks passes away on October 24th in Detroit, Michigan, leaving behind an enduring legacy.
